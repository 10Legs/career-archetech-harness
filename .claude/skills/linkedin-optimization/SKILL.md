# LinkedIn Optimization Skill

## Why LinkedIn Matters

LinkedIn is used by 87% of recruiters as their primary candidate sourcing tool. A weak LinkedIn profile means being invisible to the market. Optimization has two goals:
1. **Discoverability**: Rank in recruiter searches for your target keywords
2. **Impression**: Convert a profile view into a conversation

---

## Profile Strength Ranking (LinkedIn Algorithm)

LinkedIn suppresses incomplete profiles. Achieve "All-Star" status by completing:
- Profile photo (professional, well-lit, appropriate)
- Background banner (branded, optional but impactful)
- Headline (do NOT use just your job title)
- Location
- Industry
- Current position with description
- Education
- 5+ skills
- 50+ connections

---

## Section-by-Section Optimization

### Photo
- Professional headshot — face takes up 60% of frame
- Smile — approachable beats serious
- Background: solid, blurred, or professional setting
- Updated within last 2–3 years
- Consistent with the seniority level you're targeting

### Headline (Most Critical for Search)
LinkedIn searches heavily weight the headline. Default "Job Title at Company" is a missed opportunity.

**Formula**: `[Target Title] | [Specialty] | [Value You Deliver]`

Examples:
- "Senior Product Manager | Fintech & Payments | Building Products Users Actually Love"
- "Data Scientist | Machine Learning & NLP | Turning Messy Data Into Decisions"
- "Operations Leader | Supply Chain & Logistics | Scaling Ops From $10M to $100M"

For job seekers who want to be subtle:
- Keep current title, add specialties: "Marketing Director | Brand Strategy | B2B SaaS | Growth Marketing"

### About Section
Written in first person. 200–300 words:

**Paragraph 1**: Career identity — who you are and what you do
**Paragraph 2**: Top accomplishments (3 bullet points or flowing sentences with numbers)
**Paragraph 3**: What drives you, your philosophy, or your unique approach
**Paragraph 4**: Call to action and contact info

### Experience
- Mirror resume accomplishments but slightly more conversational
- Every role should have 3–5 bullets
- Use keywords from target job descriptions
- Media attachments: Add work samples, presentations, articles where possible

### Skills Section
- Add top 50 skills (the max)
- Top 3 skills are shown prominently — make them count
- Prioritize skills that match Tier 1 keywords from target roles
- Skills with endorsements rank higher — ask trusted colleagues to endorse

### Recommendations
- Request 2–3 recommendations from managers or senior colleagues
- More valuable than endorsements
- Provide recommenders with talking points to make their job easier

### Featured Section
Pin your best work here:
- Resume (PDF)
- Portfolio link
- Best article or post you've written
- Major project or case study
- Recognition or award

## Search Optimization (AKA "The Algorithm")

LinkedIn ranks profiles in recruiter searches based on:
1. **Keyword match** to their search terms
2. **Connection degree** (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
3. **Profile completeness** (All-Star status)
4. **Activity recency** (posting, commenting, updates)
5. **Geographic location** match

**Keyword strategy**: Include target role keywords in:
- Headline (highest weight)
- About section
- Current job title / description
- Skills section

## Open to Work Settings

**Visible to everyone (green banner)**:
- Pro: Signals urgency to all visitors
- Con: May signal desperation; alerts current employer

**Recruiters only** (recommended for employed seekers):
- Shown only to recruiters with LinkedIn Recruiter accounts
- Not visible to your connections or current employer
- Best of both worlds

**Settings to configure**:
- Job titles you're open to (use target role titles)
- Locations (include remote if applicable)
- Start date (immediately vs. within 3 months)
- Job type (full-time, contract, etc.)

## Content Strategy (for active presence)

Posting 1–2x per week dramatically increases visibility and inbound.

**Post types that perform**:
- Insight or lesson from your work (with numbers)
- Industry observation or trend commentary
- Career story (authentic + specific)
- Recommendation of a resource or tool
- Question to your network (drives comments)

**Avoid**:
- Inspirational quotes with no personal insight
- Sharing articles without commentary
- Overly promotional self-promotion
