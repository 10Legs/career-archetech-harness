# Career Strategy Patterns Skill

## Job Search Channel Effectiveness (by situation)

| Situation | Most Effective Channel |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Employed, passive | Recruiter outreach, LinkedIn (Open to Work recruiters-only) |
| Unemployed, urgent | Direct apply + Recruiter + Network simultaneously |
| Career pivot | Network first (warm referrals overcome resume gaps), then targeted apply |
| Executive search | Retained search firms, board/advisor networks, direct outreach to decision-makers |
| Early career | Campus recruiting, LinkedIn EasyApply, company career pages |
| Niche/technical | Niche job boards, GitHub, community forums, professional associations |

## The 70/30 Networking Rule

70% of jobs are filled through networking. Yet most job seekers spend 90% of their time applying online. Reverse this:
- 50% of time on networking activities
- 30% on targeted direct applications
- 20% on job board monitoring / recruiter relationships

## Outreach Templates

### Warm Reach-Out (Reconnecting)
```
Subject: Reconnecting — would love to catch up

Hi [Name],

It's been a while since [context — worked together at X, met at Y, connected through Z]. Hope you're doing well.

I'm currently exploring new opportunities in [field/role type] and would love to hear your perspective on [specific question relevant to their experience]. Even a 20-minute call would be incredibly helpful.

Would you have time for a brief chat in the next few weeks?

[Your name]
```

### Cold Outreach (Specific Target at Company)
```
Subject: [Shared context or specific hook]

Hi [Name],

I came across your profile while researching [Company] — specifically your work on [specific project/initiative from their LinkedIn]. [One sentence connecting that to why it caught your attention].

I'm a [title] with [X years] of experience in [relevant specialty], currently exploring opportunities at companies like [Company] where [specific reason you're interested — mission, product, growth].

I'd love to learn more about your experience on the [team/initiative] and hear your perspective on what makes someone successful there. Would you be open to a 20-minute call?

[Your name | LinkedIn URL]
```

### Referral Request
```
Subject: Referral request — [Role] at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I saw that [Company] is hiring for a [Role] and noticed you're connected there. I'm very interested in this position because [specific, genuine reason].

My background in [relevant skill] directly aligns with what they're looking for — [quick evidence]. I'd be very grateful if you'd be willing to pass along my resume or make an introduction.

Happy to share my resume and cover letter. Of course, no pressure at all if it's not a fit.

Thank you,
[Name]
```

## LinkedIn Strategy Patterns

### Headline Formula
```
[Target Title] | [Specialty or Industry] | [Top Outcome You Deliver]
```

Examples:
- "Product Manager | B2B SaaS | Turning User Research into Revenue-Driving Roadmaps"
- "Financial Analyst | FP&A | Building Models That Drive $100M+ Business Decisions"
- "HR Business Partner | Tech Companies | Scaling Teams From Startup to Series C"

### Activity Strategy (Passive Search)
- React + comment on posts from target company employees (adds value first)
- Post 1–2x per week sharing genuine expertise or industry insight
- Engage on recruiter posts (they notice active profiles)
- Update "Open to Work" to "recruiters only" to avoid alerting current employer

## Salary Negotiation Patterns

### Research Stack
1. Glassdoor (general market data)
2. Levels.fyi (tech companies, total comp)
3. LinkedIn Salary (industry-specific)
4. Payscale (broader market)
5. Bureau of Labor Statistics (official benchmarks)
6. Peer network (most accurate — ask directly in trusted circles)

### The Anchor-High Opening
When forced to give a number:
```
"Based on my research into comparable roles and the scope of this position, I'm targeting somewhere in the [$X–$Y] range. I'm flexible depending on the total compensation package."

Anchor at top 20% of market range. You can always come down; you can't go up.
```

### The Counter
```
"I'm very excited about this opportunity and the team. After thinking it over, I was hoping we could get to [$X] on base. Is there room to move in that direction?"

Counter at least $5–10K above offer. Never accept the first offer without at least one counter — it's universally expected.
```

### Package Negotiation Beyond Base
When base is stuck, negotiate:
- Signing bonus (often more budget available than base)
- Equity / RSUs (for private/startup: ask vesting schedule, cliff, liquidation preference)
- Performance bonus guarantee (Year 1)
- PTO + remote flexibility
- Title adjustment (affects future salary anchoring)
- Accelerated review timeline (6-month vs. 12-month for raise eligibility)
- Professional development budget / conference allowance

## Interview STAR Story Bank Template

Build 8–10 stories using this template:

```markdown
## Story: [Short Title]

**Theme**: [Leadership / Conflict / Failure / Achievement / Collaboration / Problem-Solving / Change / Learning]

**Situation**: [Context — 1-2 sentences]

**Task**: [What I needed to do / my role]

**Action**: [Specifically what I did — 3-4 steps]

**Result**: [Measurable outcome — numbers where possible]

**When to use this story**:
- "Tell me about a time you led without authority"
- "Describe a challenging stakeholder situation"
```

## Interview Research Protocol

Before every interview:
1. **Company**: Read last 3 press releases, latest earnings/investor content (if public), Glassdoor reviews (look for patterns), LinkedIn company page (recent announcements)
2. **Role**: Map every bullet in the JD to your strongest STAR story
3. **Interviewer**: LinkedIn profile, recent posts or articles, mutual connections
4. **Industry**: Any major trends, disruptions, or news relevant to their business

## Questions to Ask in Interviews (Signal Intelligence)

About the role:
- "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- "What are the biggest challenges facing this team right now?"

About the team:
- "How would you describe the team's working style?"
- "What do top performers on this team have in common?"

About the company:
- "What's the most exciting thing happening at [Company] right now?"
- "How has the company adapted to [relevant industry trend]?"

About the process:
- "What are the next steps, and what's your timeline for a decision?"
